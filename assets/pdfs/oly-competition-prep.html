Competition day is where preparation meets execution. The lifters who perform best aren't always the strongest — they're the best prepared. This guide covers attempt selection strategy, warm-up timing, and the mental framework for hitting lifts on the platform.

Attempt Selection Strategy

Warm-Up Timeline

  • 60 min before: General movement — rowing, hip circles, band work
  • 45 min before: Empty bar — snatch grip press, OHS, muscle snatch
  • 30 min before: Build snatch: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80% (singles)
  • 15 min before: Final warm-up at 85-90% of opener
  • 5 min before: Stay loose. Chalk up. Deep breaths. Visualize.

Between Snatch & C&J

  • You typically get 10-15 min between sessions
  • Stay warm — light cleans, front squats, jerk drives
  • Don't sit down for too long — keep blood flowing
  • Eat something small: banana, rice cake, electrolytes
  • Mentally reset — the snatch is done, focus forward

Competition Day Checklist

Gear & Equipment

  • Singlet (federation-approved)
  • Weightlifting shoes (heeled)
  • Belt (if you use one — practice with it)
  • Wrist wraps / tape
  • Chalk (check if venue provides)
  • Knee sleeves (check federation rules)
  • Federation card + ID
  • Towel + change of clothes

Nutrition Timeline

  • Night before: Carb-heavy dinner, hydrate well
  • 3 hrs before: Full meal — rice, chicken, banana
  • 1 hr before: Light snack — rice cake + honey
  • Between sessions: Quick carbs — gummy bears, Gatorade
  • Post-comp: Big meal + protein within 1 hour
  • Caffeine: stick to your normal amount, don't overdo it

Mental Framework — 3 Cues Per Lift

1Setup cue: "Feet set, lats tight, breathe." — Before you pull, lock in your positions. Every lift starts here.
2Pull cue: "Push the floor, stay over the bar." — Drive with legs, keep chest over bar until the power position.
3Catch cue: "Fast elbows, receive it." — Speed under the bar is the difference between making and missing.

Key Ratios to Know Before You Compete

  • Snatch : C&J ratio should be 78-82%. If your snatch is lagging, technique work is the priority — not pulling harder.
  • Clean : Front Squat ratio should be 85-90%. If your clean is well below your front squat, your pull or technique is the limiter.
  • Jerk : Clean ratio should be 95-100%. If your jerk fails more than your clean, overhead stability and footwork need work.
