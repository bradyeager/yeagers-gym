Hyrox is won in transitions and lost in pacing. The fastest athletes aren't always the fittest — they're the best at managing effort across 8 stations and 8 km of running. This playbook gives you station-specific strategy, transition tips, and a pacing framework to race smarter.

The 8 Stations — Strategy & Pacing

#StationRxTarget TimeStrategy
1SkiErg1000m3:30-4:30Long pulls, controlled pace. Don't sprint — you have 7 stations left. Arms-only temptation kills you later.
2Sled Push50m1:30-3:00Low body angle, drive with legs. Short choppy steps. Don't stop — walking pace is faster than stop-start.
3Sled Pull50m1:30-3:00Hand-over-hand, seated position. Keep tension on the rope at all times. Gloves help grip.
4Burpee Broad Jump80m3:00-5:00Biggest time sink. Consistent rhythm beats speed. Jump 4-5 ft each time. Don't sprint the jumps.
5Rowing1000m3:30-4:30Rate 24-28 spm. This is active recovery if paced right. Don't go below 2:00/500m split.

7Sandbag Lunges100m2:30-4:00Quads will be destroyed by now. Short steps, upright torso. This is a mental station — just keep moving.
8Wall Balls75/1003:00-5:00Break early: sets of 15-20. Don't go unbroken. 5-breath rest max between sets. Finish strong.

Running Pacing — The 1km Segments

Pacing Strategy

  • Run 1 (before SkiErg): 85% effort — settle in, don't race
  • Runs 2-4: Even pace at 80% — these are recovery between stations
  • Runs 5-6: Maintain pace — fatigue creeps in, stay disciplined
  • Run 7: Allow 5-10% pace drop — lungs are on fire, that's normal
  • Run 8: Leave it all on the course. Sprint the last 200m.

Transition Tips (Free Time)

  • Transitions average 15-45 seconds — pros do them in 5-10s
  • Know the venue layout — walk the course during check-in
  • Have nutrition ready: gel or chews at station 4 and 6
  • Gloves on for sled pull, off for everything else — practice this
  • Mental cue at each station entrance: "What's my break plan?"

Race Day Gear Checklist

  • Running shoes (not metcons — you're running 8 km)
  • Grip gloves or tape for sled pull
  • Race bib + timing chip (check placement rules)
  • 2-3 gels or chews (tape to shorts or bib)
  • Electrolyte bottle for pre-race hydration
  • Compression socks (optional — comfort on runs)
  • Sweatband or hat (indoor venues get hot)
  • Change of clothes + towel for post-race
  • Phone/watch for split tracking (if allowed)
  • Foam roller for post-race (venues are crowded — bring your own)

Race Day Nutrition Timeline

  • 3 hrs before: Full meal — rice, chicken, banana. Nothing new.
  • 1 hr before: Small snack — rice cake + honey, or a banana
  • 15 min before: Sip electrolytes, caffeine if you use it (100-200mg)
  • Mid-race: Gel at station 4 (burpee broad jumps) and station 6 (farmers carry)
  • Post-race: Protein + carbs within 30 min. Hydrate aggressively.

Time Targets by Division

  • Podium (Open): Sub 1:05:00 (M) / Sub 1:15:00 (F)
  • Competitive: 1:10-1:25:00 (M) / 1:20-1:35:00 (F)
  • Solid finish: 1:25-1:40:00 (M) / 1:35-1:50:00 (F)
