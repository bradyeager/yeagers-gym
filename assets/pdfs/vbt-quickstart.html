VBT replaces guesswork with data. Instead of asking "how did that feel?" you ask "how fast did that move?" Bar speed tells you how strong you are today, when to add weight, when to stop a set, and when to go home. This guide gets you from zero to tracking in your first session.

Your First VBT Session — Step by Step

1 →

Pick One Lift

Start with squat, bench, or deadlift. One lift. Don't try to track everything on day one. Master the process before expanding.

2 →

Build Your Baseline

Work up from 50% to 90% in 5-6 sets. Record velocity at each load. This creates your load-velocity profile — your personal strength fingerprint.

3 →

Learn the Zones

>0.75 m/s = speed work. 0.55-0.75 = working sets. 0.40-0.55 = strength zone. <0.40 = near-max. These are your training guardrails.

4 →

Track One Metric: First-Rep Velocity

Every session, note the velocity of your first rep at your working weight. If it trends up over weeks, you're getting stronger. That's it. Start simple.

5 →

Add Velocity Loss After 2 Weeks

Once you're comfortable: set a velocity loss cutoff (e.g., 20%). When your bar speed drops 20% from rep 1, stop the set. This auto-regulates your volume.

The Core Concepts — Cheat Sheet

ConceptWhat It MeansWhy It Matters
Mean VelocityAverage speed across the full concentric phasePrimary metric for load selection and zone targeting
Peak VelocityFastest instantaneous speed during the liftUseful for power/explosive training, less for strength
Velocity Loss %How much slower the last rep is vs. the firstMeasures fatigue — your automatic stop signal
e1RMEstimated 1RM calculated from velocity + loadKnow your max every session without testing it
MVTMinimum velocity threshold — your slowest possible successful repThe speed at which you'd fail. Individual to each lift.

30-Day Milestones

  • Week 1: Set up device, track velocity on warm-ups and working sets
  • Week 2: Build load-velocity profiles for your main lifts
  • Week 3: Start using velocity zones to select working loads
  • Week 4: Add velocity loss cutoffs to auto-regulate volume
  • By day 30 you'll have enough data to estimate your 1RM from any set

Mistakes to Avoid

  • Tracking every exercise — start with 1-2 barbell compounds
  • Obsessing over peak velocity — mean velocity is your primary metric
  • Ignoring slow days — the data is telling you something, listen
  • Not recording data — velocity without a log is a wasted data point
  • Using VBT on machines or cables — it's designed for free weights
