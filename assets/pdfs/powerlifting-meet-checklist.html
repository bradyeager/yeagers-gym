Attempt Selection Strategy

Squat / Bench / Deadlift — Same Logic

Attempt 1 Something you can triple any day. ~88-90% of gym max. Goal: go 1-for-1. Build confidence. Get on the board.
Attempt 2 A solid single — challenging but certain. ~95-97%. This is your real working attempt. Most of your total is built here.
Attempt 3 A PR attempt or a smart jump based on how 2nd moved. ~100-103%. Only go big if attempt 2 was smooth.

If Your Opener Felt Heavy

  • Take a conservative 2nd — repeat or add only 2.5 kg
  • Don't chase numbers that aren't there today
  • A 7/9 or 8/9 day beats a 4/9 hero attempt day
  • Protect your total — going 9-for-9 on first meet is ideal

Velocity Cue for Attempt Selection

  • If opener velocity > 0.35 m/s — you're strong today, jump normally
  • If opener velocity 0.25-0.35 m/s — moderate day, conservative 2nd
  • If opener velocity < 0.25 m/s — grinder. Repeat or small jump only
  • Use warm-up velocity to predict opener difficulty before you're on platform

Equipment Checklist

  • Singlet (federation-approved)
  • T-shirt (no logos/graphics on some feds)
  • Long socks for deadlift
  • Belt (10mm or 13mm, IPF-approved if needed)
  • Wrist wraps
  • Knee sleeves (check thickness rules)
  • Flat shoes (Converse, wrestling shoes, or deadlift slippers)
  • Chalk (loose or liquid — check venue)
  • Federation membership card
  • Government-issued ID
  • Snacks: rice cakes, bananas, gummy bears, PB packets
  • Electrolyte drink (Gatorade, LMNT, Pedialyte)
  • Foam roller or lacrosse ball
  • Resistance bands for warm-up
  • Headphones + charger
  • Cash (some venues are cash-only for spectators/merch)

Warm-Up Protocol

  • General: 5 min low-intensity + mobility
  • Empty bar x 5, 40% x 3, 55% x 2, 70% x 1, 80% x 1
  • Final warm-up: 85-90% of opener x 1
  • Time your last warm-up to finish ~5 min before you lift
  • Keep warm between flights — band work, light movement

Post-Meet Recovery

  • Day of: eat a big meal, hydrate, celebrate
  • Days 1-3: light walking only, lots of sleep
  • Days 4-7: easy movement — 50% loads, no grinders
  • Week 2: return to normal training volume at reduced intensity
  • Write down what went well and what you'd change
