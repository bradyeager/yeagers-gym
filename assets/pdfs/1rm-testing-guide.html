Testing a true 1RM is a skill, not just loading plates until you can't. Proper setup, smart jumps, and knowing when to call it are the difference between a clean PR and an injury. This guide covers when to test, how to ramp, and how the estimation formulas compare to a true max.

When to Test vs. When to Estimate

Test a True 1RM When

  • You're peaking for a competition (2-3 weeks out)
  • You haven't tested in 12+ weeks and estimates feel stale
  • You need precise numbers for a new training cycle
  • You have a spotter/coach and proper equipment
  • You're well-rested, well-fed, and feeling strong

Estimate Instead When

  • Mid-training block — max attempts disrupt recovery
  • You're in a caloric deficit or sleep-deprived
  • You have a nagging injury or joint issue
  • You're a beginner — rep PRs build more skill
  • VBT data can estimate 1RM without the fatigue cost

The 1RM Testing Protocol

From Empty Bar to Max Attempt — Step by Step

1General warm-up: 5 min cardio + dynamic mobility for the lift.
2Empty bar x 8, then 50% x 5, 60% x 3, 70% x 2, 80% x 1 (of estimated max).
385% x 1 — this should feel moderate. Gauge bar speed and confidence.
490-92% x 1 — should be challenging but clean. Rest 3-4 min.
595-97% x 1 — heavy single. If it moves well, you have room. Rest 4-5 min.
6100-103% x 1 — PR attempt. Full focus. One shot. If it's there, it's there.
7If successful and clean: optional 2nd attempt at +2.5-5 lbs. Max 3 true attempts total.

Day-Before Checklist

  • 7+ hours of sleep the night before
  • Eat a full meal 2-3 hours before testing
  • Hydrate with electrolytes starting the day before
  • No heavy training 48-72 hours prior
  • Caffeine OK — stick to your normal amount

Safety Rules

  • Always use safeties or a competent spotter
  • Know your bail-out: how to dump a squat, roll a bench
  • Never max out alone on bench press
  • If the 90% single felt like a grinder — stop there
  • 3 max attempts is the absolute limit per session

Post-Test Recovery

  • Max testing is high-CNS stress — treat it like a meet
  • Next 2-3 days: light movement only
  • Resume training at 70-80% intensity for the first week
  • Expect to feel "off" for 3-5 days — that's normal
  • Log your numbers immediately — they fade fast

Pro Tip — Use Submaximal Estimation

  • A clean set of 3 at RPE 8.5 predicts your 1RM within 2-5% without the recovery cost
  • VBT gives you an estimated 1RM every session — no need to grind for it
  • The best athletes test rarely and estimate often — save your maxes for the platform
