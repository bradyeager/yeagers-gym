Zone Velocity Range ~RPE Training Use Coaching Cue
Speed-Strength > 0.75 m/s 5 - 6 Warm-ups, speed work, power development, technique drilling "Fast and explosive — this should feel light."
Strength-Speed 0.55 – 0.75 m/s 6.5 - 7.5 Working sets, volume accumulation, hypertrophy with intent "Controlled but aggressive. Every rep should look the same."
Strength 0.40 – 0.55 m/s 8 - 8.5 Heavy working sets, strength blocks, top singles/doubles "This is real weight. Stay tight and push through."
Absolute Strength 0.25 – 0.40 m/s 9 - 9.5 Near-max efforts, peaking, competition prep, 1-3RM territory "Grind zone. If it moves, you've got it."
1RM / Max Effort < 0.25 m/s 10 True max attempts, competition lifts, testing days only "All or nothing. Full send."

When to Push Harder

  • Bar speed faster than expected at working load
  • Velocity trending UP across warm-up sets
  • RPE feels lower than the velocity zone suggests
  • Readiness score is high (sleep, soreness, energy)

When to Pull Back

  • Velocity drops >10% from set 1 to set 3
  • Bar speed in warm-ups slower than usual
  • Grinding reps at loads that normally move well
  • Poor sleep, high soreness, or low energy

Mid-Session Decision Tree

Velocity > target zone → Add 5-10 lbs or add a set. You're stronger today.
Velocity in target zone → Stay the course. Execute the program as written.
Velocity below target zone → Drop 5-10% load or cut volume. Protect recovery.
Velocity drops >20% in set → Stop the set. Fatigue has exceeded productive training stimulus.

Important Notes

  • Velocity zones are lift-specific — squat zones differ from bench zones by ~0.05-0.10 m/s
  • These ranges are for mean concentric velocity on compound barbell lifts
  • Build your personal load-velocity profile to calibrate these zones to YOUR body
