Training is the stimulus. Recovery is where adaptation happens. If you're not recovering well, more training makes you worse, not better. This guide covers the readiness scoring system, the recovery hierarchy, and exactly what to do on green, yellow, and red days.

Daily Readiness — The 5 Signals

Score Interpretation — What to Do Today

8-10 GreenPush day. Train as programmed. Consider adding weight or a set if warm-ups feel fast. This is your strongest training day.
5-7 YellowMaintain day. Train at 85-90% of programmed intensity. Keep volume the same but reduce load 5-10%. Don't skip — just adjust.
0-4 RedRecovery day. Light movement only — walk, mobility, foam roll. No heavy lifting. Extra sleep and nutrition focus. Come back stronger tomorrow.

The Recovery Hierarchy

Tier 1 — Non-Negotiable

  • Sleep: 7-9 hrs. This is #1. Everything else is secondary.
  • Nutrition: Adequate protein (0.8-1g/lb) and calories
  • Hydration: Half your bodyweight in oz, minimum
  • These three account for 80% of your recovery capacity
  • No supplement, gadget, or protocol replaces these

Tier 2 — High Impact

  • Stress management: 10 min daily breathwork or meditation
  • Active recovery: 20-30 min easy walk or light movement
  • Training load management: Don't exceed MRV
  • Deload weeks: Every 4-6 weeks, reduce volume 40-50%
  • These amplify Tier 1. Useless without it.

Tier 3 — Nice to Have

  • Cold exposure: 2-5 min cold plunge post-training
  • Sauna: 15-20 min, 3-4x/week for circulatory benefits
  • Massage / soft tissue: Weekly or as needed
  • Compression boots: Convenient, marginal effect
  • These are cherry-on-top. Fix Tier 1 and 2 first.

Sleep Optimization Checklist

Environment

  • Room temp 65-68°F (18-20°C)
  • Pitch black — blackout curtains or eye mask
  • No screens 30-60 min before bed
  • Consistent bedtime ±30 min, even weekends
  • White noise or earplugs if needed

Nutrition for Sleep

  • Last meal 2-3 hours before bed
  • Include carbs at dinner — they help serotonin production
  • Caffeine cutoff: 8-10 hours before bedtime
  • Magnesium glycinate (200-400mg) before bed if needed
  • Alcohol destroys sleep quality — even 1-2 drinks

Tracking Your Readiness Over Time

  • Log your daily readiness score for 4+ weeks — patterns emerge
  • If you're yellow/red more than 3x in a week, your training load or lifestyle needs adjustment
  • Pair readiness scores with warm-up velocity data for the most complete picture
  • Correlate readiness trends with training performance — you'll learn YOUR recovery timeline
