Nutrition Periodization Guide

Match Your Nutrition to Your Training Phase

YEAGER'S GYM
yeagersgym.com

Eating the same way year-round is like training the same way year-round — suboptimal. Your nutrition should change as your training goals change. Building muscle? You need a surplus. Cutting for a meet? You need a deficit. Maintaining? You need precision. This guide shows how to phase your nutrition alongside your programming.

The 4 Nutritional Phases

Protein Priorities — The Non-Negotiable Macro

Protein Targets by Goal

Muscle gain0.8-1.0 g/lb bodyweight. Surplus means you don't need to overdo protein — carbs are the fuel.
Maintenance0.8-1.0 g/lb bodyweight. Enough to preserve, not enough to stress about hitting exactly.
Fat loss1.0-1.2 g/lb bodyweight. Higher protein protects muscle in a deficit. This is the most important phase to hit protein.
Competition0.8-1.0 g/lb bodyweight. Don't over-eat protein pre-competition — it's thermogenic and can affect weight.

Transition Rules Between Phases

  • Never jump from surplus to aggressive deficit — taper down over 2 weeks
  • After a cut: reverse diet back to maintenance over 3-4 weeks
  • Spend at least 4 weeks at maintenance between cuts
  • Don't bulk above 18-20% body fat — you'll spend too long cutting
  • Longer, moderate phases beat short, aggressive phases every time

Signs You Need a Diet Break

  • Weight stalled for 2+ weeks despite adherence
  • Sleep quality declining, energy tanking
  • Training performance dropping consistently
  • Extreme hunger, food obsession, or binge urges
  • Take 1-2 weeks at maintenance calories, then resume

Meal Timing for Athletes

Training Day Template

  • Pre-training (2-3 hrs): Balanced meal — protein + carbs + moderate fat
  • Pre-training (30 min): Quick carbs if needed — banana, rice cake, honey
  • Intra-training: Water + electrolytes. Carb drink if session >90 min.
  • Post-training (within 1 hr): Protein (30-40g) + fast carbs
  • Evening meal: Include carbs — they improve sleep quality

Rest Day Template

  • Total calories same as training day (or slightly lower carbs)
  • Don't dramatically cut calories on rest days — you're still recovering
  • Spread protein across 4-5 meals (25-40g per meal)
  • Fat can be slightly higher on rest days — it's satiating
  • Hydration is just as important — don't slack because you're not sweating

The 80/20 Rule

  • 80% of your results come from total calories and protein. Get those right first.
  • Meal timing, carb cycling, and supplement stacks are the last 20% — don't optimize these until the basics are locked in
  • Track for 2-4 weeks to calibrate, then transition to intuitive eating with periodic check-ins
