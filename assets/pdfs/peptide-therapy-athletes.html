Peptides are short chains of amino acids that signal specific biological processes — recovery, tissue repair, fat metabolism, and sleep quality. They're not steroids, and they're not magic. But when combined with proper training, nutrition, and sleep, they can meaningfully accelerate recovery and support longevity goals. Here's what athletes should actually know.

Who Benefits Most

  • Athletes 35+ dealing with slower recovery
  • Nagging tendon/ligament injuries that won't resolve
  • Lifters in caloric deficit losing sleep quality
  • Anyone prioritizing longevity alongside performance
  • Post-surgery recovery (with physician clearance)

What Peptides Won't Do

  • Replace training, nutrition, or sleep
  • Build muscle on their own (they're not anabolic steroids)
  • Fix problems caused by overtraining or under-eating
  • Work without consistency — protocols require weeks
  • Compensate for poor programming or garbage volume

Safety & Sourcing

Sourcing Guidelines

  • Only use compounding pharmacies or licensed providers
  • Avoid "research chemical" peptides — no quality control
  • Third-party COA (Certificate of Analysis) is mandatory
  • Peptides should arrive lyophilized (freeze-dried powder)
  • Store reconstituted peptides refrigerated, use within 30 days
  • Brad consults with clients on vetted sourcing options

Reconstitution Basics

  • Use bacteriostatic water (BAC) — never sterile water
  • Inject BAC slowly along vial wall — don't spray the powder
  • Swirl gently — never shake reconstituted peptides
  • Use insulin syringes (29-31 gauge) for subcutaneous injection
  • Rotate injection sites: abdomen, thigh, love handles
  • Use the calculator at yeagersgym.com/tools-peptide.html

Stacking with Training

Recovery Stack Example

  • BPC-157 + TB-500 for injury rehab (synergistic)
  • CJC/Ipamorelin nightly for systemic recovery + sleep
  • Time GH-releasing peptides 30+ min after last meal
  • Don't inject GH peptides near high-sugar meals
  • Track readiness scores — peptides should improve trends

Body Comp Stack Example

  • Semaglutide or tirzepatide for appetite management
  • Pair with adequate protein (1g/lb minimum)
  • Strength training is non-negotiable — preserve muscle
  • Monitor for GI side effects — dose escalation matters
  • Regular lab work every 8-12 weeks
