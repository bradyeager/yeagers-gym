The Problem

Most remote coaching flies blind.

The problem with most remote coaching

Most remote coaching works like this: you get a program. You train. You fill out a check-in form once a week. Your coach reads it, maybe adjusts a few things, sends next week's program.

The problem? The coach only sees what you report. And self-reported data is limited.

"How did the set feel?" is a useful question. But "How fast did the bar move?" is a better one.

The Solution

The solution to effective remote coaching.

Velocity data bridges the gap

What if your coach could see exactly how every set moved — how fast the bar traveled, when fatigue kicked in, and whether today was a push day or a pull-back day? That's what velocity-based remote coaching gives you. Instead of relying on subjective check-ins, your bar speed tells the whole story.

Your programming adjusts week to week based on real performance data — the same objective feedback Brad uses with in-person athletes. Distance doesn't dilute the coaching. Data closes the gap.
